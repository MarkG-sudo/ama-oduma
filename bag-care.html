Bag Care Guide

How to maintain and protect your textile print bags

About Textile Print Bags

Our bags are handcrafted using African print fabrics (Ankara), which are vibrant, symbolic, and made for both beauty and durability. With the right care, they can last for many years without fading or losing their shape.

Cleaning Tips

  • Spot Clean Only: Use a clean, damp cloth with a mild soap solution. Gently dab stains instead of rubbing.
  • No Machine Washing: Avoid putting Ankara bags in washing machines to preserve their shape and print quality.
  • Dry Immediately: If the bag gets wet, air dry in a shaded area. Do not use a dryer or place in direct sunlight as this can fade the colors.
Always test a small hidden area before applying any cleaning solution to check for colorfastness.

Storage Advice

  • Keep Shape: Stuff your bag with paper or fabric to retain its original form when not in use.
  • Avoid Plastic: Store in a breathable cloth or dust bag. Plastic can trap moisture and cause mildew.
  • Cool, Dry Space: Store away from sunlight and damp areas to protect fabric and linings.

General Handling Tips

  • Rotate Your Bags: Give it time to rest and recover its shape.
  • Mind Where You Place It: Don’t place your bag on the floor or dirty surfaces. Use a hook or clean surface when out.
  • Avoid Heavy Loads: Overloading your bag can strain the seams and damage the fabric.
“Proper care of textile print bags honours both the art of African textiles and the sustainability movement.” — Fabric Conservator’s Note

Why It Matters

Maintaining your bag protects your investment in quality craftsmanship and promotes sustainable fashion. These small efforts contribute to a longer-lasting and more eco-friendly wardrobe.
